Trip Destination Visualization

This visualization displays predicted trip destination density in the Edmonton Central Metropolitan Area in 2065.

Each dataset contains trip data predictions according to a city plan.

Analysis

Each dot represents 1-300 trips to a particular zone in the city. Their size is scaled according to max(0.1, (trips / 300)).

To visualize the trip density, points are assigned a random latitude-longitude pair that falls within the destination zone of the trip(s) that they represent.

Controls

The controls on the left side of the app can be used to choose the dataset and filter the data on screen.

To change the colours of the dots or overlays, right-click an entry and a colour-chooser will pop up.

Future Work

Right now, the dots are randomly assigned to points within their destination zones. However, some of these points end up in nonsensical areas like bodies of water.

Future iterations of this tool will use a more accurate zone boundary file that will limit the latitude-longitude assignments to developed areas.
